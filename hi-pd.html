PD: My name is MacKenzie, and I really want to work with you!

For real, though. I even made you this webpage.

This is me ^

I'm a graphic design student in my final year of studies in Ottawa, Canada. I love early mornings, black coffee, and most importantly, YouTube reaction and analysis videos.

Apart from my regular design work, I also dabble in coding. I'm still building this site, so you're getting a sneak preview-- no extra charge. If you check out the Projects page, you'll find a few examples of my work. Feel free to look around!

Great design is all about empathy.

If you can place yourself in your audience's shoes, you have the chance to create something that resonates.

Making something look good is just a small part of design; my real goal as a designer is to make things, that make you feel things.

If you take a look at my work, you'll see I have a lot to say. Making work intentionally — based on real data and user insights — is extremely important to me. Accessibility is huge too.

Don't get me wrong. I love the final stages of design where you see the work finally come together; but my focus is making use of opportunities to meaningfully connect clients and their users.

Life is about fleeting moments. Reaction videos are the closest thing we have to recapturing and re-experiencing emotions.

— some guy probably

So why should you hire me?

I'll be real with you, PD. I'm a UI / UX, branding, strategic marketing, web design, photo-retouching kinda girl.

If you're looking for someone who's really into illustration, animation, and motion graphics, I'm not the designer for you.

But, if you've come this far, even if you don't need my skillset right now, let me give you a few more reasons to keep me in mind.

I'm a still a student.

I know it would be dope to have 10+ years of industry experience, but I'm not there (yet). What I can tell you is I'm flexible, enthusiastic, and extremely receptive to feedback. I've still got lots of room to grow as a designer, so what you see isn't my best work-- it's just where I've been so far.

Also, did I mention I'm Canadian? Let those currency conversion rates work in your favour!

Remote work is kind of the best.

I can't say a global pandemic was the ideal way to find out I like working from home, but it turns out, I'm actually pretty good at it. If I have clear tasks and deadlines, I get things done. I'm good with email, Zoom, Slack, Google Drive; wherever & whatever you're using, I can accommodate.

I jive with your content.

If you look at the case studies I've written so far, you'll see that I've consistently fallen into projects for K-pop and YouTube. I can't say they make me perfectly qualified for the job, but I do have at least some background to inform design work in those contexts.

I also have a background in music, which is part of the reason I was first drawn to your channel. I know music production and performance aren't identical, but between that and a genuine interest in K-pop and K-culture, I've tagged along for a lot of your work. Your videos have given a lot to me, and I'd love an opportunity to give you something back.

I value people who value their craft.

The truth is that you work on cool projects, have neat ideas, and I want to be a part of it. I have a lot of respect for the way you construct your ideas, and how you generally conduct yourself.

Hearing you talk about why you started making videos, and why you'll keep going despite content blocks, is why I decided to apply for this. Even if my work isn't your cup of coffee, I hope you'll hold onto the fact that some chick in Canada is sincerely cheering you on as a fellow creator.

If reaction videos can have cultural significance so, too, can design work. And if design is anything like my favourite movies, it's better when the experience is shared with someone else.

Let's make something significant, together!
